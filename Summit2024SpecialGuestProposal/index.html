Lifting as We Climb

Powerful conversations, challenging topics, sisterhood that uplifts and inspires.

The Smithsonian’s National Museum of African American History and Culture’s (NMAAHC) Women’s Summit is dedicated to three values: the empowerment, entrepreneurship, and engagement of African American women of all ages.

Since it began in 2018, the Summit has evolved into a signature event that welcomes 350 established and emerging professional women who participate on site at the Museum and over 3,000 viewers who engage virtually.

In 2024, participants will explore the history, legacy, and future of “Lifting as We Climb,” Mary Church Terrell’s apocryphal call for Black women to raise up other members of their communities as they forge new paths to achieve racial equity and social justice.

“Look at what [our ancestors] did. Now imagine what you can do.”

Oprah Winfrey

Inaugural NMAAHC Women’s Summit
June 7, 2018

Your Role in Lifting as We Climb

On behalf of Kevin Young, the Andrew W. Mellon Director of the Smithsonian’s National Museum of African American History and Culture, I would like to invite you to be a featured participant in our 2024 Women’s Summit on Monday, March 11. One of the Museum’s signature events, the Summit is dedicated to exploring three values: the empowerment, entrepreneurship, and engagement of African American women of all ages.

Beginning with Oprah Winfrey at our inaugural event in 2018, the Women’s Summit has featured such illustrious speakers as Cathy Hughes, founder and chair of Urban One Inc.; Alexis Herman, former U.S. Secretary of State; Beverly Bond, founder of BLACK GIRLS ROCK!®; Lisa Price, founder of Carol’s Daughter; as well as noted politician, voting rights advocate, and author Stacey Abrams.

Using the theme “Lifting as We Climb,” the 2024 Summit will highlight formidable African American women who use their knowledge and professional success to mentor and uplift today’s emerging leaders. We strongly believe that your work continues to have a profound professional and social impact that embodies the Summit’s “three Es.” We would be honored if you would consider participating in a panel discussion with other phenomenal women to share your stories, offer advice, and inspire Summit attendees.

The 2024 Women’s Summit will be held from 8:30 to 11:30 a.m. at the National Museum of African American History and Culture in Washington, D.C. In addition to participating in a moderated, 30-minute panel discussion with at least three other African American women, we also ask that you participate in a brief networking session with attendees. To help you prepare, we will provide a general list of questions related to your background, professional inspirations, and reflections on advice from mentors who helped guide your path to success.

We appreciate your consideration of this invitation and are tremendously excited by the prospect of your participation. We will follow up on this correspondence within the next two weeks with the hope of securing your positive response. In the meantime, if you have any questions, I am happy to speak with you or anyone on your team to facilitate your gracious acceptance of this invitation.
