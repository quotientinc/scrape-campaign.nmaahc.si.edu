Lifting as We Climb

Powerful conversations, challenging topics, sisterhood that uplifts and inspires.

The Smithsonian’s National Museum of African American History and Culture’s (NMAAHC) Women’s Summit is dedicated to three values: the empowerment, entrepreneurship, and engagement of African American women of all ages.

Since it began in 2018, the Summit has evolved into a signature event that welcomes 350 established and emerging professional women who participate on site at the Museum and over 3,000 viewers who engage virtually.

In 2024, participants will explore the history, legacy, and future of “Lifting as We Climb,” Mary Church Terrell’s apocryphal call for Black women to raise up other members of their communities as they forge new paths to achieve racial equity and social justice.

A Signature Event Since 2018

The National Museum of African American History and Culture is the only national museum devoted exclusively to the documentation of African American life, history, and culture. Since opening its doors in 2016, the Museum has welcomed over 10 million visitors on site and millions more virtually. The inaugural Women’s Summit took place in 2018.
